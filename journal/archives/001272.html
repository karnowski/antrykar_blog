So, Where Have I Been?

I could just say "away" and be done with it, but I don't think I'll do that. There's a lot to talk about since the last time I posted, and I'm not even counting that last "Wow it's been a long time" thing, either.

To keep it as brief as possible, I've been putting quite a bit of time and energy into musical projects over the last year or so. I'm teaching drums and percussion part time at a local music shop and have been playing with several groups around Knoxville and the surrounding areas including Cheating Spoon, Leslie Woods and Dark Mountain Orchid, and occasional gigs with the Doug Harris Project. I've still been working with Ron in Citizen X, though we haven't been as productive as normal. This is largely due to the fact that Leslie and I moved into a new house in West Knoxville. It took a while for us to get things situated, and since then the days have been more and more hectic.

I've had a few fits of trying to get back into writing, but my time and energy levels have been so drained that I haven't really been able to put much into other than the occasional article for Blount Today. I've started reworking The Sacred Magicks of Elrinm the Mage, which I am now calling simply: Myths of Thera - Book I. Who knows if I'll keep that title or not, but it's as good as any at this point. I've got a decent start on it, and I can feel myself getting excited about the story, which is a good sign, I think.

You know, only two paragraphs later and I feel pretty good about having summed up the last year. I plan on making this a regular thing again. I think I may have finally found the balance I've needed in order to follow both my musical and literary paths...
